Negative Effects of Blue-light

Computers, phones, tablets, and televisions are our main sources of electronic light.
Of the wavelengths of visible light, blue-light has been attributed to increasing risk of retinal cell death. Blue light that has high energy and a short wavelength, and due to the photoelectric effect, it can remove more electrons off of molecules and damage the eye by changing its oxygen concentrations (). In addition to eye-strain, studies have shown that light-emitting electronics before bedtime can have a signficant impact on sleep, morning alertness, melatonin suppression, and the circadian clock. A shifted circadian clock would be like having jet-lag: the body feels like it is not aligned with our daily schedules. Ultimately, this would decrease the amount of sleep one would receive, increasing stress, loss of function, and a shortened life expectancy.
From the scientific journals that I have researched, most of them demonstrate that these negative effects are not as visible and harmful in short-term studies (6 months to 1.5 years). For most of these, however, a longitudinal study does not exist. In a world where technology has perpetuated every aspect of our life, the long-term consequences of blue-light should be studied. We don't know how constant blue-light and shifts in our circadian rhythm will effect aging, eye-sight, and stress. One could say that we could compare the eye-tissue and health of people who heavily use screen electronics with those who have not, but there are so many confounding factors such as age, natural sunlight, genetics.

What is Night-Shift Mode?
"Asymmetrical Virtual Reality" is where player interaction is split into two channels: the desktop and the VR kit. In this project game, one player uses the desktop to control one entity and the VR player will battle the desktop player. The desktop player will throw fruits and have mobility whereas the the VR player will have no mobility, but the ability to smash the fruits and throw their only weapon at the desktop player. This project is intended to expand the interactiveness of VR, allowing both the singular VR players and those observing outside of VR to both have fun simulataneously.
