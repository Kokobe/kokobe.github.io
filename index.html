Asymmetrical Fruit Ninja
"Asymmetrical Virtual Reality" is where player interaction is split into two channels: the desktop and the VR kit. In this project game, one player uses the desktop to control one entity and the VR player will battle the desktop player. The desktop player will throw fruits and have mobility whereas the the VR player will have no mobility, but the ability to smash the fruits and throw their only weapon at the desktop player. This project is intended to expand the interactiveness of VR, allowing both the singular VR players and those observing outside of VR to both have fun simulataneously.

BIO

My name is Jehan Kobe Chang.

I am currently a freshman undergraduate at UC Irvine majoring in Computer Science. I am also in the Campuswide Honors Program (CHP) -- which has me write blogs about the social, technological impacts of Empire and its Ruins.

Here is my resume and if you would like to know more about me, my email is jkobechang@gmail.com or Facebook message me @ Kobe Chang.

When I was a junior attending Henry M. Gunn High School, I founded and presided over the Gunn Virtual Reality Club. A year later, I published my VR game onto Steam . Now, I am a Programming Officer for VR @ UCI.

I have had two paid summer internships at Amplify.ai This company specializes in "AI-driven conversations" and makes Facebook chatbots for companies and campaigns such as Cheetos, Sephora, 48 Months, Flawless, etc.

Artificial Intelligence's infinite applications stimulate my creativity and enable me to supercharge my projects. I aim to develop projects that utilize AI's capabilities in generating positive, social, technological, and environmental impacts on the world (with a little VR thrown into the mix).
